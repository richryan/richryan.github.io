Financial Economics, Winter 2016

Materials for Financial Economics, ECON 435, with Professor Pablo Ottonello

Notes

  1. What an interest rate is, the value of money over time, annuities, perpetuities, bonds
  2. Present value, future value, internal rate of return, effective annual rate, annual percentage rate, coupon rate, zero-coupon bond, yield to maturity
  3. Bonds continued, stocks
  4. Valuing stocks
  5. Happy Labor Market Friday, Exam 1 solutions
  6. Random variables, variance, standard deviation, diversification in stock portfolios
  7. The volatility of a large portfolio, risk-free saving and borrowing
  8. The efficient portfolio, CAPM
  9. Identifying a stock’s \(\alpha\), Fama–French–Carhart
  10. Capital structure, unlevered equity, levered equity, Modigliani–Miler
  11. MM continued, Vandelay Industries, interest tax shield
  12. Happy Labor Market Friday, \(V^{\text{L}} = V^{\text{U}} + \text{PV}(\text{interest tax shield})\), financial options, call option, put option, option writer, strike or exercise price, American option, European option, long and short positions, option premium, open interest, at-the-money, in-the-money, out-of-the-money, deep in-the-money or out-of-the-money
  13. Put–call parity, \(S + P = \text{PV}(K) + C\), \(\text{PV}(K) = K - D(K)\)
  14. A call option graphically, more put-call parity, thanks
