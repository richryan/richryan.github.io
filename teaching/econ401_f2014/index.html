Intermediate Microeconomic Theory, Fall 2014

Section notes for Intermediate Microeconomic Theory, ECON 401, with Professor Tilman Börgers

  1. Happy Labor Market Friday, valuing time, the rationality hypothesis, the transitive property, choices and consistent rankings
  2. CES utility functions, convex preferences, depicting indifference curves
  3. Checking rationality continued, demand functions, Cobb–Douglas preferences, monotonic transformations, more CES preferences
  4. Income and substitution effects, in pictures, numerically calculating the income and substitution effects, the sign of the substitution effect
  5. Marshalian demand, elasticities, Hicksian (compensated) demand, verifying that the Slutsky equation holds
  6. Comparative statics: prices, complements and substitutes, perfect complements, perfect complements, slightly imperfect substitutes, slightly imperfect complements
  7. Hicksian demand with perfect substitutes, labor supply, labor supply with perfect substitutes, labor-supply elasticity
  8. Firms’ profit maximization, isoquants, cost minimization, cost minimization with Cobb–Douglas technology, conditional factor demand functions
  9. Cost functions and returns to scale, \(p \geq \min \text{AC}(q) \), returns to scale and Cobb–Douglas
  10. Exam review, quadratic marginal cost, circle isoquants
  11. Equilibrium supply and demand, taxes, it doesn’t matter who pays the tax, taxation with linear demand and supply, nonlinear demand and elasticities: \[ \frac{d p}{d \tau} = \frac{\eta}{\eta + \varepsilon}\]
  12. Equilibrium in several interacting markets, partial equilibrium
  13. Equilibrium in several markets continued, short- vs long-run cost
  14. The change in consumer surplus, the slope of the demand curve, compensating variation, equivalent variation, for normal goods: \[ \text{EV} < \text{CS loss} < \text{CV}, \] for quasilinear utility: EV = CV
  15. Markets and efficiency; EV = old prices, new utility; CV = new prices, old utility; with transfers, all agents prefer equilibrium to all other allocations; equivalent and compensating variation with perfect complements and linear utility
  16. Tax incidence analysis: deadweight loss, Professor Börgers’s milk farm, Arrow’s Impossibility theorem, the Condorcet cycle
  17. Monopoly, the markup: \[\frac{P(Q)}{\text{MC}(Q)} = \frac{1}{1- 1/ |\varepsilon|}, \] first-degree price discrimination
  18. Price discrimination: first-, second-, and third-degree price discrimination, sale in different markets, \[p_1 > p_2 \iff |\varepsilon_1| < |\varepsilon_2|, \] Professor Börgers’s cat farm
  19. A very brief introduction to game theory, Nash equilibrium, chicken, modified picnic, modified traffic, beauty contest
  20. Game theory continued: Bertrand and Cournot, Braess’s paradox, Bertrand with differentiated products
  21. Risk and uncertainty, lotteries, St. Petersburg paradox, concavity and risk aversion, von Neumann Morgenstern utility function
  22. Externalities, a negative externality in consumption, strategic interaction and externalities
  23. The picnic example continued, moral hazard, thanks
